# Annual Compliance Overview

Maintaining compliance is an ongoing responsibility for all businesses in Cameroon. This checklist helps you stay on track throughout the year.

## Monthly Requirements

### Tax Declarations

- **DSF (Déclaration Simplifiée des Flux)**: Due by the 15th of each month
- **Sales records**: Maintain detailed sales logs
- **Expense records**: Keep all purchase receipts and invoices
- **Payment**: Pay any taxes due by the deadline

**Action Items:**
- Set monthly reminders for the 15th
- Organize receipts by month
- Review bank statements monthly
- File even if you had no sales (nil return)

## Quarterly Requirements

### VAT Declarations (if applicable)

- Submit VAT returns if your business is VAT-registered
- Calculate input and output VAT
- Pay any VAT due

### CNPS Contributions

- Submit employee contribution reports
- Pay monthly CNPS contributions
- Update employee information as needed

## Annual Requirements

### Tax Annual Return

- **Deadline**: Usually by March 31st of the following year
- Submit annual tax return (Déclaration Annuelle)
- Include all supporting documents
- Pay any outstanding tax liabilities

### Patente Renewal

- **When**: Annually, typically in the first quarter
- Renew your municipal business license
- Pay the annual patente fee
- Update business information if changed

### RCCM Updates

- Update RCCM if business details changed
- Report changes in:
  - Business address
  - Management structure
  - Business activities
  - Capital changes

### Financial Statements

- Prepare annual financial statements
- Have accounts audited (if required)
- Submit to relevant authorities
- Keep copies for your records

## Common Compliance Mistakes

1. **Missing deadlines**: Set up calendar reminders
2. **Incomplete records**: Keep all receipts and documents
3. **Not filing nil returns**: File even with no activity
4. **Outdated information**: Keep business details current
5. **Ignoring penalties**: Address issues immediately

## Penalties and Consequences

### Late Filing
- Financial penalties
- Interest on outstanding amounts
- Potential business closure

### Non-Compliance
- Legal consequences
- Business license suspension
- Difficulty accessing services

## Best Practices

### Document Management
- Keep all receipts organized
- Maintain digital backups
- Store documents for at least 5 years
- Use accounting software

### Stay Informed
- Monitor regulatory changes
- Subscribe to tax authority updates
- Attend compliance workshops
- Consult professionals when needed

### Proactive Approach
- Don't wait until deadlines
- Review compliance monthly
- Address issues early
- Plan for busy periods
