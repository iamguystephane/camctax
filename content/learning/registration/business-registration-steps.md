# Getting Started

Registering a business in Cameroon involves several steps and government agencies. This guide walks you through the process.

## Choose Your Business Form

Before registration, decide on your business structure:

- **ETS (Entreprise Unipersonnelle à Responsabilité Limitée)**: Single-person business with limited liability
- **SARL (Société à Responsabilité Limitée)**: Limited liability company (2+ partners)
- **SA (Société Anonyme)**: Public limited company (requires more capital)

Each form has different requirements and capital requirements.

## Registration Process

### Step 1: Name Reservation

- Check name availability at the Trade and Personal Property Credit Register (RCCM)
- Reserve your business name (valid for 3 months)
- Cost: Approximately 5,000 XAF

### Step 2: Prepare Required Documents

You'll need:

- National ID or passport
- Proof of address (utility bill or lease agreement)
- Business plan (for some business forms)
- Capital deposit proof (for SARL and SA)

### Step 3: Register at RCCM

- Submit your registration file at the RCCM office
- Pay registration fees (varies by business form)
- Receive your RCCM extract (usually within 5-10 business days)

### Step 4: Obtain Tax Identification

- Apply for NIU (Numéro d'Identification Unique) at the tax office
- Present your RCCM extract
- Receive your taxpayer card

### Step 5: Register for Patente

- Apply for municipal business license at your local council
- Pay annual patente fee (varies by location and business type)
- Required for most commercial activities

### Step 6: CNPS Registration (if applicable)

- Register with CNPS if you have employees
- Submit employee information
- Set up monthly contribution payments

## Common Challenges

- **Delays**: Government processing can take longer than expected. Start early.
- **Document requirements**: Requirements may vary by region. Check with local offices.
- **Language**: Some forms may be in French. Consider getting help if needed.

## Tips for Success

- Start with name reservation early
- Keep copies of all documents
- Follow up regularly on your application status
- Consider professional help for complex business structures

## Next Steps

Once registered, you'll need to:

- Open a business bank account
- Set up accounting records
- Register for VAT if applicable
- Understand your tax obligations
